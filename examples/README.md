## üí° Key Learnings from These Examples

### 1. **Go Beyond Surface-Level**
Don't just list features - analyze:
- Who are they targeting?
- What pain points do they solve?
- How do they position themselves?
- Where are the gaps?

### 2. **Pricing is Critical**
Always document:
- Pricing model (subscription, freemium, usage-based)
- All tiers and what's included
- Free trial details
- Annual vs monthly options
- What's NOT included in each tier

### 3. **Find the Weaknesses**
Every competitor has weaknesses:
- Limited free plans
- Missing features
- Poor UX in certain areas
- Lack of transparency
- High pricing
- Slow support

### 4. **Identify Opportunities**
The goal is to find where YOU can win:
- Underserved market segments
- Feature gaps
- Better pricing structure
- Faster onboarding
- Superior support

### 5. **Use Evidence**
Back up claims with:
- Specific pricing numbers
- Review scores from G2/Gartner
- Quotes from their website
- Customer counts
- Feature lists

## üìä Report Sections Explained

### Overview
High-level summary: What they do, who they target, company stage

### Pricing
Detailed breakdown of all plans, pricing model, and business model

### Features
Not just a list - organize by category, highlight differentiators

### Messaging & Positioning
HOW they talk about themselves: tone, target audience, pain points addressed

### Social Proof
Evidence of success: customers, reviews, awards, certifications

### Design & UX
Website analysis: design aesthetic, navigation, user experience, CTAs

### Marketing & Growth
How they acquire customers: content, SEO, partnerships, events

### Support & Community
Post-sale experience: support channels, documentation, community

### Strengths
What they do exceptionally well (be honest, don't minimize)

### Weaknesses
Where they fall short (specific, not vague)

### Key Takeaways
The synthesis: What matters? What can you learn? Where can you compete?
