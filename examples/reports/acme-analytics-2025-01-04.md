## Overview

Acme Analytics is a mid-market business intelligence platform targeting SMBs with 50-500 employees. They focus on self-service analytics with AI-powered insights. Founded in 2020, they've raised $15M Series A and claim 2,000+ customers.

**Tagline:** "Analytics so simple, anyone can use it"

**Core Value Proposition:** Turn your business data into actionable insights without needing a data team.

---

## Pricing

**Model:** Freemium + Tiered Subscription

### Free Plan - $0/month
- 1 user
- 1 data source connection
- 5 dashboards
- 30-day data retention
- Community support only

### Starter Plan - $49/month
- Up to 5 users
- 3 data source connections
- Unlimited dashboards
- 90-day data retention
- Email support
- Basic AI insights

### Professional Plan - $149/month (Most Popular)
- Up to 15 users
- Unlimited data sources
- Unlimited dashboards
- 1-year data retention
- Priority email support
- Advanced AI insights
- Custom reports
- API access

### Enterprise Plan - Custom Pricing
- Unlimited users
- Dedicated account manager
- White-label options
- SSO/SAML
- 24/7 phone support
- Custom integrations
- SLA guarantees

**Free Trial:** 14-day trial on Professional plan, no credit card required

**Payment:** Monthly or annual (2 months free with annual)

## Features

### Core Features
1. **Drag-and-drop dashboard builder** - No SQL required
2. **AI-powered insights** - Automatic anomaly detection and trend analysis
3. **100+ pre-built templates** - Industry-specific dashboard templates
4. **Real-time data sync** - Updates every 5 minutes
5. **Mobile app** - iOS and Android apps for on-the-go analytics

### Data Connections
- Supported sources: Google Analytics, Stripe, Salesforce, HubSpot, PostgreSQL, MySQL, Google Sheets, Excel
- Custom API connections available on Enterprise plan
- OAuth-based authentication for security

### Collaboration
- Share dashboards with view-only or edit access
- Comments and annotations on charts
- Scheduled report delivery via email
- Slack integration for alerts

### AI Features (Beta)
- Natural language queries ("Show me sales by region last month")
- Predictive analytics for forecasting
- Automated insights ("Sales dropped 15% in the Midwest - investigate?")

## Strengths

1. **Simple, intuitive UX** - Truly no-code, easy for non-technical users
2. **Affordable pricing** - Competitive pricing for SMB market
3. **Strong free trial** - 14 days, no credit card = low friction
4. **AI features** - Natural language queries are differentiated
5. **Good social proof** - Strong review scores and recognizable customers
6. **Mobile apps** - Native apps give them an edge over web-only competitors

---

## Weaknesses

1. **Limited free plan** - Only 1 user makes it hard to try with a team
2. **Data retention** - 30-90 days is short for historical analysis
3. **Enterprise features lacking** - White-label and SSO only on custom pricing
4. **Integration ecosystem** - Only ~20 integrations vs competitors with 100+
5. **AI features in beta** - "Beta" label may reduce trust
6. **No phone support** - Even Professional plan is email-only

---

## Key Takeaways

### What They Do Well
- Clear positioning for SMB market
- Low-friction trial and onboarding
- Strong value prop ("analytics without complexity")
- Good balance of features vs simplicity

### What They're Missing
- Advanced analytics for power users
- Deeper integrations (they focus on breadth, not depth)
- Data governance features for compliance
- On-premise deployment options

### Competitive Differentiation
- **vs. Tableau/Power BI:** Much simpler, more affordable, but less powerful
- **vs. Google Analytics:** Broader data sources, not just web analytics
- **vs. Other SMB tools:** AI-powered insights and natural language queries

### Opportunities for Your Product
1. Offer longer data retention on lower tiers
2. Provide phone support at Professional level
3. Build deeper integrations vs just API connections
4. Focus on data governance if targeting regulated industries
5. Offer more generous free plan (allow teams to try together)
